# Crux Triggers

## Purpose
Cruxes are moments where the player's current understanding is no longer enough.
They push the player from routine execution into anticipatory play.

## Strong cruxes for this game

### A monitor contradiction
The camera says one thing, the soundscape suggests another.

### A malfunction during calm
A system begins failing before any confirmed breach.

### A breach on the wrong route
The attack ignores the expected lane.

### Missing confirmation
A sensor that should ping does not.

### A delayed radio response
The neighboring node answers too late, too vaguely, or not at all.

### A defense performs differently than expected
A turret misses, overheats, jams, or reveals a blind spot in its lane.

### Two minor failures combine
A small power issue and a quiet camera turn out to be the start of one larger event.

### A transit objective interrupts a routine defense plan
The player must suddenly prioritize passage or preservation.

### A salvage opportunity appears at the wrong moment
Greed and prudence conflict directly.

### A fallback system is needed earlier than planned
A contingency becomes the present.

## What good cruxes do
They should make the player ask:
- What is actually happening here?
- Which interpretation is right?
- What matters most now?
- What am I willing to lose?

## Crux design rule
A crux should not merely make the game harder.
It should force the player to revise their understanding.
