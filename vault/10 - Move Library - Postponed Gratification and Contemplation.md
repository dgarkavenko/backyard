# Move Library — Postponed Gratification and Contemplation

## Core idea
A major reward in this game is watching prior preparation become present stability.

## Moves

### Set up a defense with no immediate proof it was smart
**Why:** preparation is faith under uncertainty.  
**Accomplishes:** creates delayed payoff potential.  
**Cost / risk:** may never matter if predicted wrongly.

### Wait and watch instead of intervening too early
**Why:** the player wants to see whether the system can hold.  
**Accomplishes:** preserves attention and trust in preparation.  
**Cost / risk:** delayed intervention can become costly.

### Recognize that earlier prep is saving you
**Why:** this is the emotional payoff of the game.  
**Accomplishes:** produces earned satisfaction.  
**Cost / risk:** none; this is the reward moment.

### Watch automated defenses solve the problem you predicted
**Why:** confirmation of mastery.  
**Accomplishes:** creates calm and competence.  
**Cost / risk:** overconfidence may follow.

### Notice that automation buys time to think
**Why:** free attention is a reward.  
**Accomplishes:** opens room for second-order planning.  
**Cost / risk:** the player may miss a new escalation while enjoying success.

### Resist overmanaging a functioning defense
**Why:** trust is part of expertise.  
**Accomplishes:** preserves bandwidth.  
**Cost / risk:** can feel risky in a tense game.

### Use the quiet seconds after stabilization to prepare the next response
**Why:** safety is temporary.  
**Accomplishes:** converts reward into readiness.  
**Cost / risk:** minimal pause for reflection.

### Take satisfaction in system survival, not enemy death
**Why:** reinforces intended role.  
**Accomplishes:** keeps the game from drifting toward shooter fantasy.  
**Cost / risk:** requires presentation and feedback to support it.

### Read the aftermath to understand why it held
**Why:** payoff should create learning.  
**Accomplishes:** supports mastery and future anticipation.  
**Cost / risk:** time cost.

### Feel pride in an ugly improvised setup that worked
**Why:** competence under scarcity is central to the game.  
**Accomplishes:** gives emotional ownership to imperfect success.  
**Cost / risk:** none; this is a key emotional reward.
