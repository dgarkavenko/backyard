# Open Design Questions

## Scope
- How much of the neighboring node is explorable?
- Is transit always abstracted, or occasionally embodied?
- How many defense devices can the player meaningfully manage without drifting into RTS texture?

## Threat model
- Do enemies adapt behaviorally, ecologically, or only route-wise?
- Can malfunctions be caused indirectly by enemy pressure, contamination, or both?
- How readable should enemy intention be before contact?

## Failure and recovery
- What exactly constitutes local degradation?
- How much loss is recoverable through labor vs through future supply?
- When does a soft-fail spiral become a hard fail?

## Information design
- Which cues are trustworthy?
- How often should radio information be wrong, partial, or late?
- How much camera and sensor friction is ideal before it becomes annoying?

## Emotional tone
- How much explicit horror versus restrained sci-fi dread?
- Is the neighboring line a source of comfort, guilt, or denial?
- Does the station feel merely old, or actively dying?

## Identity
- Does the player remain purely an operator, or ever become a field scavenger in a bigger way?
- What makes this station distinctly yours?

## Next useful note
A strong next step would be:
- a list of **station systems**
- a list of **enemy pressure patterns**
- a list of **phase structures** for one full shift
