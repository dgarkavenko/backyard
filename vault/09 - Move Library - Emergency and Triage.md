# Move Library — Emergency and Triage

## Core idea
These moves define the worst-moment feel of the game.

## Moves

### Interrupt a planned task because a new alarm overrides it
**Why:** emergencies seize priority.  
**Accomplishes:** immediate response.  
**Cost / risk:** original task remains half-done.

### Choose which failure becomes real
**Why:** two crises cannot both be solved in time.  
**Accomplishes:** explicit triage.  
**Cost / risk:** one loss becomes authored and memorable.

### Run to the malfunction instead of the breach
**Why:** system collapse may be the deeper threat.  
**Accomplishes:** preserves infrastructure.  
**Cost / risk:** physical incursion may deepen.

### Run to the breach instead of the malfunction
**Why:** immediate containment matters most.  
**Accomplishes:** preserves local safety.  
**Cost / risk:** hidden systemic damage accumulates.

### Patch under fire
**Why:** clean downtime does not exist.  
**Accomplishes:** preserves critical function.  
**Cost / risk:** high personal vulnerability.

### Use a crude emergency fix
**Why:** partial function now beats proper repair later.  
**Accomplishes:** keeps the system alive.  
**Cost / risk:** instability or repair debt later.

### Trigger a defense manually
**Why:** automation failed or confidence collapsed.  
**Accomplishes:** regains immediate control.  
**Cost / risk:** increases operator burden.

### Sacrifice future salvage to stop present spread
**Why:** containment outweighs economy.  
**Accomplishes:** stabilizes the present.  
**Cost / risk:** poorer recovery after the fight.

### Abandon a planned harvest because the line is no longer safe
**Why:** caution interrupts greed.  
**Accomplishes:** preserves life and schedule.  
**Cost / risk:** missed material gain.

### Lock a sector down while assets remain outside
**Why:** containment is now more important than completeness.  
**Accomplishes:** prevents wider collapse.  
**Cost / risk:** meaningful loss.

### Power down one subsystem to keep the relay alive
**Why:** central function is sacred.  
**Accomplishes:** preserves the most important capability.  
**Cost / risk:** blindness or weakness elsewhere.

### Decide before having enough information
**Why:** delay has become more dangerous than error.  
**Accomplishes:** restores momentum.  
**Cost / risk:** low-confidence action.

### Realize two small failures are one large event
**Why:** the player's model catches up too late.  
**Accomplishes:** creates a memorable crux.  
**Cost / risk:** panic or costly reprioritization.

### Feel guilty for what you knowingly let die
**Why:** triage should leave residue.  
**Accomplishes:** deepens meaning of future preparation.  
**Cost / risk:** none mechanically, but high experiential value.
