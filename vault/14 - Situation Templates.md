# Situation Templates

These are reusable structures for playful situations built from clusters of moves.

## 1. Uneasy routine
**Setup:** ordinary maintenance window.  
**Moves:** perimeter walk, preventive maintenance, radio listening, trust or distrust instruments.  
**Crux:** a small inconsistency appears.  
**Payoff:** player reinterprets the whole station mood.

## 2. False confidence
**Setup:** all signs point to one breach route.  
**Moves:** narrow-lane defense setup, confirmation seeking, overcommitment.  
**Crux:** attack starts elsewhere or the expected attack was only partial.  
**Payoff:** player learns to hedge and distrust obvious readings.

## 3. Prepared reward
**Setup:** earlier investment in a lane, trap, or maintenance cycle.  
**Moves:** trust automation, watch instead of intervene, use freed attention elsewhere.  
**Crux:** a secondary complication emerges while the main defense holds.  
**Payoff:** delayed gratification and competence fantasy.

## 4. Ugly save
**Setup:** simultaneous breach and malfunction.  
**Moves:** choose which failure becomes real, patch under fire, emergency fix, abandon outer node.  
**Crux:** costs become permanent.  
**Payoff:** survival without victory.

## 5. Recovery under debt
**Setup:** bad previous night left the sector degraded.  
**Moves:** cannibalize systems, postpone upgrades, choose future weakness.  
**Crux:** not enough time to restore everything.  
**Payoff:** authored asymmetry and long-term adaptation.

## 6. Calm that feels wrong
**Setup:** unusually quiet shift.  
**Moves:** watch wrong-seeming sectors, treat absence as data, listen to machinery, interpret radio tone.  
**Crux:** first sign of failure arrives indirectly.  
**Payoff:** anticipation becomes the core play.

## 7. Transit interruption
**Setup:** the sector has a temporary logistics objective.  
**Moves:** rebalance defense, protect infrastructure over self, hold lane integrity.  
**Crux:** the route weakens during passage.  
**Payoff:** objective-specific pressure without larger scope.

## 8. Greed versus caution
**Setup:** valuable salvage is available outside.  
**Moves:** risk salvage run, maintain line coverage, choose whether to leave assets outside.  
**Crux:** a new signal arrives mid-harvest.  
**Payoff:** the player authors whether economy or stability matters more.

## 9. System trust break
**Setup:** automated defense previously felt reliable.  
**Moves:** delegate to automation, focus elsewhere.  
**Crux:** automation behaves unexpectedly.  
**Payoff:** the player becomes more interpretive, less complacent.

## 10. End-of-night triage
**Setup:** attack is over but the station is compromised.  
**Moves:** assess damage, salvage, choose repair order, postpone something valuable.  
**Crux:** there is not enough downtime before the next phase.  
**Payoff:** recovery becomes the real strategic layer.
