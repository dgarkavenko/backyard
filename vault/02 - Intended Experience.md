# Intended Experience

## Experience statement
The player should feel like an isolated reserve-line operator whose competence is expressed through ritual upkeep, anticipatory reading of weak signals, and the ability to recover from partial collapse rather than prevent all damage.

## Experience pillars

### 1. Loneliness and maintenance task
The player spends meaningful time alone with systems, rituals, checklists, and ambient signals.

### 2. Lack of resources
Scarcity should shape priorities, not merely limit shopping.
The player should often think:
- I cannot do everything
- I have to choose what becomes vulnerable
- fixing this means neglecting that

### 3. Postponed gratification
Preparation should feel uncertain when performed and satisfying later when it pays off.
The player should often think:
- I hope this setup is enough
- I planned for this
- the system is holding because I prepared earlier

### 4. Contemplation of automated defense
Watching the defense work is part of the reward.
The player should sometimes step back and observe:
- a lane holding
- a trap paying off
- automation buying time to think

### 5. Tension and constant anticipation
The threat should often be suspected before it is confirmed.
The player should often think:
- something is wrong
- this is too quiet
- the real breach may be elsewhere

### 6. Emergencies at the worst possible moment
Malfunctions and attacks should overlap in ways that force triage.
The player should often think:
- I cannot solve both
- which failure do I allow to become real?
- I am surviving, but the outpost is getting worse

## Experience anti-goals
The game should avoid drifting into:
- power fantasy gunplay
- frictionless base optimization
- fully predictable wave-defense mastery
- reset-heavy fail states that erase the meaning of recovery

## Design check
A system belongs if it makes the player feel:
- strained
- vigilant
- competent through discipline
- quietly proud when ugly systems hold

A system does not belong if it mainly makes the player feel:
- dominant
- overarmed
- mechanically detached from consequences
