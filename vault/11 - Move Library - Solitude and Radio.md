# Move Library — Solitude and Radio

## Core idea
These moves make the wider network feel present without expanding the playable scope.

## Moves

### Answer the radio immediately
**Why:** contact is rare and valuable.  
**Accomplishes:** preserves continuity with the wider line.  
**Cost / risk:** interrupts urgent local work.

### Let the radio wait
**Why:** the line comes first.  
**Accomplishes:** keeps focus on immediate survival.  
**Cost / risk:** delayed information or emotional distance.

### Treat instructions as incomplete
**Why:** command may not understand your local reality.  
**Accomplishes:** turns narrative into interpretation.  
**Cost / risk:** misreading intent.

### Talk back even when nobody answers
**Why:** ritual resists isolation.  
**Accomplishes:** deepens role and tone.  
**Cost / risk:** no direct system gain.

### Project meaning onto a distant light or signal
**Why:** the player wants proof the network still exists.  
**Accomplishes:** creates hope, dread, or false security.  
**Cost / risk:** interpretation may be wrong.

### Read the outpost as a place shaped by previous operators
**Why:** infrastructure implies history.  
**Accomplishes:** turns environment into narrative residue.  
**Cost / risk:** none mechanically.

### Continue the routine because routine itself keeps panic away
**Why:** ritual is emotional maintenance.  
**Accomplishes:** stabilizes player mindset and role identity.  
**Cost / risk:** routine can delay adaptation.

### Realize you are maintaining the line as much for yourself as for command
**Why:** duty becomes personal.  
**Accomplishes:** deepens meaning of survival.  
**Cost / risk:** none mechanically.
