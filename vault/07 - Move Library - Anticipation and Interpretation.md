# Move Library — Anticipation and Interpretation

## Core idea
These moves produce suspense, predictive play, and the feeling that weak signals matter.

## Moves

### Suspect the obvious route is a feint
**Why:** the player has learned to read intent, not only contact.  
**Accomplishes:** changes posture before proof exists.  
**Cost / risk:** may misallocate defense.

### Watch one monitor too long
**Why:** seek certainty.  
**Accomplishes:** deep local information.  
**Cost / risk:** other areas are neglected.

### Leave a camera on a quiet sector because quiet feels wrong
**Why:** silence becomes meaningful.  
**Accomplishes:** keeps ambiguity in focus.  
**Cost / risk:** less information elsewhere.

### Rank likely breach vectors mentally
**Why:** simplify chaos into a model.  
**Accomplishes:** enables quicker decisions.  
**Cost / risk:** fixation on the wrong theory.

### Read a previous pattern into tonight
**Why:** the player wants continuity and learnability.  
**Accomplishes:** supports prediction.  
**Cost / risk:** false pattern recognition.

### Treat a malfunction as a signal, not an accident
**Why:** the threat may work through systems.  
**Accomplishes:** reorders priorities.  
**Cost / risk:** can overstate coincidence.

### Listen to radio chatter for implication
**Why:** tone can matter more than explicit instruction.  
**Accomplishes:** narrative becomes tactical inference.  
**Cost / risk:** ambiguity remains.

### Delay action to gather one more confirming signal
**Why:** avoid overreaction.  
**Accomplishes:** increases confidence.  
**Cost / risk:** can make response late.

### Act on a hunch before confirmation
**Why:** anticipation outruns proof.  
**Accomplishes:** allows foresight to matter.  
**Cost / risk:** strong chance of wasted effort.

### Treat absence as data
**Why:** dead air and missing pings can mean failure.  
**Accomplishes:** deepens system reading.  
**Cost / risk:** interpretation can drift into paranoia.

### Wonder whether the neighboring node is still active
**Why:** the player models the wider line.  
**Accomplishes:** contextualizes all later signals.  
**Cost / risk:** uncertainty can distort planning.

### Feel dread because the line is too calm
**Why:** calm may imply upstream collapse.  
**Accomplishes:** justifies over-preparation or caution.  
**Cost / risk:** can lead to inefficient play.
