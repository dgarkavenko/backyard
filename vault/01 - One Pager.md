# One Pager

## Working title
**Dead Relay**

## Format
Single-player, first-person survival-defense with base maintenance, light crafting, monitoring, and soft-fail progression.

## Premise
You are the operator of a reserve relay tower on a secondary frontier line. The main line absorbs most of the pressure elsewhere. At first, only rare breaches reach your sector. When they do, it means something upstream has already failed.

You maintain one isolated section of the system:
- communications relay
- monitoring coverage
- safe transit through a narrow corridor
- local perimeter integrity

The enemy is a hostile techno-organic force. It does not attack primarily to kill you. It attacks to break the function of the sector: blind monitors, damage relays, corrupt infrastructure, and open a path deeper into the line.

## Core fantasy
Keep a failing system alive for one more night.

## Pillars
- loneliness through routine
- vulnerability over empowerment
- defense as maintenance
- delayed gratification through preparation
- soft failure and recovery
- mystery through infrastructure and radio traffic

## Play space
- tower interior
- immediate perimeter
- exposed outer zone
- narrow tunnel or service corridor toward one neighboring node

## Loop
### Prepare
Inspect, repair, reposition monitors, scavenge, craft, place defenses, decide what not to fix.

### Endure
Track threats, respond to breach patterns, manually stabilize failing systems, choose what to save.

### Recover
Assess damage, salvage components, restore partial function, interpret radio chatter, prepare under worsened conditions.

## Main resources
- **Parts**
- **Time**

## Failure model
Failure should be mostly **degradational**, not instantly binary.

### Local failure
You survive, but lose a system, node, asset, or lane.

### Sector degradation
Coverage shrinks, supply weakens, recovery worsens, future nights become harder.

### Cascading crisis
More systems require manual handling, confidence drops, the line becomes brittle.

### Hard fail
Only when the sector can no longer perform its basic function.

## Hook
A first-person survival-defense game where you maintain a forgotten relay tower on a dying frontier line, fighting not for victory, but to keep a failing system alive one more night.
